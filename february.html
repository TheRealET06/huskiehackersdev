February Schedule

The Huskie Hackers program, by the Office of Innovation at Northern Illinois University, is a unique initiative to support and develop student talent through active participation in the Major League Hacking (MLH) Hackathon season.

Week 1 (February 3 - 7): Introduction to AI/ML

Wednesday, February 5 (3 PM - 5 PM):

  • Overview of AI and Machine Learning: Discuss key concepts, terminology, and real-world applications.
  • Introduction to Python for AI/ML: Ensure all members are comfortable with Python, the primary language used in AI/ML projects.

Thursday, February 6 (3 PM - 5 PM):

  • Exploring AI/ML Tools and Libraries: Introduce essential libraries such as TensorFlow, Keras, and scikit-learn.
  • Setting Up Development Environments: Assist members in configuring their systems with necessary tools and environments.

Friday, February 7 (2 PM - 4 PM):

  • Kick-off for Global Hack Week: Discuss the schedule, objectives, and expectations for the upcoming week.
  • Team Formation: Organize members into teams based on interests and skills.

Week 2 (February 10 - 14): Global Hack Week Participation

Wednesday, February 12 (3 PM - 5 PM):

  • Project Development: Teams work on their chosen projects, with mentors available for guidance.

Thursday, February 13 (3 PM - 5 PM):

  • Mid-Week Check-In: Teams present progress updates and discuss challenges faced.
  • Problem-Solving Session: Collaboratively address any technical or conceptual issues.

Friday, February 14 (2 PM - 4 PM):

  • Final Touches: Teams finalize their projects, focusing on debugging and optimization.
  • Presentation Preparation: Prepare for project demonstrations and submissions.

Week 3 (February 17 - 21): Post-Hack Week Reflection and Advanced Topics

Wednesday, February 19 (3 PM - 5 PM):

  • Project Presentations: Teams showcase their projects to the group, followed by Q&A sessions.

Thursday, February 20 (3 PM - 5 PM):

  • Introduction to Advanced AI/ML Topics: Explore areas like deep learning, natural language processing, or computer vision.

Friday, February 21 (2 PM - 4 PM):

  • Brainstorming Session: Identify new project ideas incorporating advanced AI/ML concepts.

Week 4 (February 24 - 28): Advanced Project Development

Wednesday, February 26 (3 PM - 5 PM):

  • Project Planning: Define objectives, scope, and timelines for new projects.

Thursday, February 27 (3 PM - 5 PM):

  • Initial Development: Working on advanced projects, setting the stage for further development.

Friday, February 28 (2 PM - 4 PM):

  • Demo Day: Project Demonstrations.
